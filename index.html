Welcome Home to Emu Road

Escape the everyday in a home that offers the perfect blend of relaxation, nature, and convenience.

Coastal adventures

Bribie Island's pristine beaches and Sandstone Point's waterfront events are just a short drive away.

Gardens, fernery & established fruit trees

Established gardens and a tranquil fernery bring seasonal colour. Productive orchard includes: 2× mulberry, lemon, tangelo, mandarin, 2× orange, kaffir lime, Tahitian lime, finger lime, and a lime×cumquat hybrid. Enjoy big‑sky sunsets and rainbows from the patio, with a generous covered alfresco area for effortless outdoor entertaining.

Birdlife, Wildlife & Outlook

Reserve backdrop with abundant native wildlife — kangaroos grazing, frogs, and a rich array of birdlife. Regular native visitors: corellas, rainbow lorikeets, black & sulphur-crested cockatoos, magpies, and kookaburras.

Why we have loved living here

This is more than a home — it's a retreat. We treasure the sense of space and privacy, the peaceful mornings filled with birdsong, and the crystal-clear starry skies at night. The coast is close enough for a spontaneous swim, yet the city is within easy reach when needed.

Location, privacy, tranquillity

Outside an estate and neighbouring only two properties, this home enjoys a quiet reserve outlook — more space, fewer fences, and room for the gardens to breathe.

  • Reserve-side privacy; minimal passing traffic
  • Mature trees & fernery provide natural screening
  • Sunset through the gums; golden afternoon light

Why South East Queensland — and why now

Greater Brisbane remains undersupplied with strong population growth and constrained new builds. The 2032 Olympics are accelerating infrastructure, while lifestyle migration from southern states continues. Quality acreage and family homes are set to stay in demand.

  • Low comparable stock in the acreage segment
  • Ongoing lifestyle migration to SEQ (value vs Sydney/Melbourne)
  • Buyer confidence improving as rates stabilise
